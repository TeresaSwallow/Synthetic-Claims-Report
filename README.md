# Synthetic-Claims-Report
Excel-based analysis of synthetic healthcare claims data, including data transformation, Pivot Tables, visualization, and claim status reporting.

-------------------------------------------------------

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Analyze a synthetic healthcare dataset to evaluate the distribution of claim payment outcomes and identify patterns in claims processing status.

-------------------------------------------------------

DATASET

Randomly generated synthetic healthcare claims dataset on Kaggle

URL: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/abuthahir1998/synthetic-healthcare-claims-dataset

-------------------------------------------------------

TOOLS

* Excel
* Pivot Tables
* Pie Charts
* Data Cleaning and Transformation

-------------------------------------------------------

PROCESS

* Imported CSV dataset from Kaggle.

* Reviewed CSV dataset, validated data types, and prepared records for aggregation using Pivot Tables.

* Created a Pivot Table to calculate total number of claims by payment status.

* Generated visualization (Pie Chart) to show Status of Claims.

-------------------------------------------------------

FINDINGS

Paid claims represented the largest category at 36% of total claims.

Denied claims accounted for 33% of total claims, while Partially Paid claims represented 31%.

Claim outcomes were distributed relatively evenly across all three statuses, with no single status significantly outweighing the others.

The balanced distribution suggests that the dataset was designed to represent variety of claim outcomes rather than accurately reflect the operational performance of a real healthcare organization. 

-------------------------------------------------------

LIMITATIONS

* Analysis focused exclusively on operational reporting of claim status distribution and did not examine relationships between claim outcomes and other variables, such as Insurance Type, Diagnosis Code, or Reason Code.

* The dataset is synthetic and may not reflect real-world healthcare claims patterns. 

* Analysis did not include predictive modeling or trend analysis spanning multiple years.
